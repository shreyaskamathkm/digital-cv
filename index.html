About Me

Hello! I am Shreyas Kamath, an individual driven by a profound passion for technology and innovation.

I am a passionate computer vision and machine learning engineer with over 8 years of experience pushing the boundaries of artificial intelligence across academic and industry settings. AI and deep learning are more than just career interests for me â€“ they are lifelong passions.

In industry roles at companies like SimpliSafe and American Science and Engineering, I worked on developing mission-critical technologies leveraging computer vision and deep learning. At SimpliSafe, I was part of the Computer Vision Analytics team focusing on AI solutions for home security systems, cameras, and smart locks. I developed in-house object detection models based on YOLO to enhance surveillance capabilities and reduce gaps in existing systems. Applying techniques like data augmentation and model compression, I was able to optimize these AI systems to significantly improve mean average precision while minimizing latency.

At American Science and Engineering, I had the opportunity to create AI technologies for security threat detection as a Computer Vision & Deep Learning Intern. I successfully built deep learning models to identify contraband in complex X-ray scanner images. To improve model training, I leveraged generative adversarial networks to synthesize realistic contraband images. I found it extremely rewarding to work on AI innovations that have such a positive societal impact in fields like security.

In my spare time, I stay updated on the latest advancements in artificial intelligence and computing. I enjoy reading research papers and blog posts about innovations in deep learning, computer vision, and machine learning. For example, I have published 5 journal papers and 15 conference papers during my academic research at Tufts University. I also practice expanding my skills by analyzing datasets from Kaggle competitions related to image classification and object detection. Additionally, I work on creative coding projects such as building a simple image classification model to identify bird species from photographs I took while hiking. Experimenting with new algorithms and datasets helps me continuously improve as a computer vision engineer. I believe maintaining this lifelong learning perspective is key for any successful engineer in the rapidly evolving field of AI.

Beyond the technical skills, I pride myself on being an innovative thinker and collaborative team member. I enjoy mentoring other engineers and sharing my passion for AI. My strengths include quickly mastering new technologies, uncovering creative solutions, and communicating complex concepts clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences.

Of course, I make sure to explore interests outside of engineering as well, which actually stimulates my creativity. When I'm not coding, you can often find me hiking, taking photographs, traveling, and exploring various cuisines. I enjoy geeking out on science fiction books, films, and TV shows that imagine futuristic societies shaped by technology. My diverse perspectives and experiences fuel the innovative solutions I bring to the table.
